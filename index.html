R2

Computational Redistricting — Geospatial ML at Census Scale

SF EXPLORATORIUM · DEC 2026 · UCSD CSE Honors Thesis

An end-to-end pipeline that generates and scores U.S. congressional redistricting plans over Census-2020 blocks — 158K to 5.5M blocks per state, 5–18M-row Parquet corpora. Three intentionally distinct generators (greedy k-means++ & Kernighan–Lin, a Sequential Monte Carlo sampler, and XGBoost + pymetis graph partitioning at pooled favored-share MAE ~0.042 across nine states, Texas held out) compete head-to-head. I authored the fairness metrics — VRA Section-2 Gingles tests, Polsby–Popper compactness, the efficiency gap — and the framework reproduces ground-truth district counts for AL, CA & TX.

XGBOOST · PYMETIS · SEQUENTIAL MONTE CARLO · GEOPANDAS · FAIRNESS METRICS

Laboratory for Emerging Intelligence, UCSD — Teradata-funded

— Lessons learned

What the work taught me

  • A demo is a hypothesis; production is the proof.

    Migrating AI Voice Agents to Lex V2 only mattered once it ran live with Disney+. "It works on my machine" is the start of the work, not the finish.

  • Research earns its keep when it meets reality.

    On LifeSaver, diagnostic accuracy meant nothing without emergency-grade latency — the model had to be right and fast for someone who actually needed the answer.

  • Constraints are the design, not the obstacle.

    California's 5.5M blocks dictated which algorithms were even admissible. Holding Texas out kept me honest about whether I'd built something real or just fit to what I could see.

  • Invest at the boundary, not the center.

    The agentic platform lives or dies on a fault-graceful executor and clean MCP servers — not a clever prompt. When 7,000 users feel a failure, graceful degradation is the feature.

  • Lead so the thing outlasts you.

    Scoping clearly and getting out of the way — from a 6-intern team to a 7,000-student federation — is how communities are built to outlast any one founder.

— Principles

How I work

  • Ship to real users, not demos.

    A model matters once it's in production — a live Disney+ deployment, an AI Tutor for 8,000 students.

  • Aim research at people.

    Health, civic, education — where the output changes a life, not just a benchmark.

  • Build communities.

    Talent compounds when you give it a place to gather. So I keep founding them.

  • Bias to agency & ownership.

    I'd rather own the outcome than wait for permission.

  • Range over silos.

    From C++ PID controllers to LoRA fine-tuning — hard problems don't respect department lines.

  • Depth plus rigor.

    Range only counts if each layer is done right. Competition — robotics, debate, USACO — set the bar.
